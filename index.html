About me

I am a Ph.D. student in the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering and Center for Machine Learning at Georgia Tech. I'm very fortunate to be advised by Mark Davenport and Chris Rozell, and to be supported by the NDSEG Fellowship.

My technical research involves developing methods for statistical and causal inference that exploit low-dimensional and temporal structure using tools from statistics, signal processing, and machine learning. My past and current projects involve (1) Bayesian methods for exploiting temporal and low-dimensional structure in inverse problems; (2) theory and algorithms for causal inference that exploit low-dimensional models; and (3) causality and low-dimensional structure in explainability and interpretability.

My long-term career goal is to help align advancements in science and technology with societal interests. I believe policy plays a cruical role in this, and plan to pursue a career in policy after graduating. I'm a current fellow in the Sam Nunn Security Program, where my research involves societal impacts of machine learning and disinformation.

Outside of research, I enjoy hiking and cycling, music (especially classical), and baking. I have an only slightly tenuous claim to an Erdös-Bacon-Sabbath number of nine: my Erdös number is 4, my Bacon number (from a role as an extra in a major movie) is 2, and my Sabbath number (from my time as a violist) is 3.
