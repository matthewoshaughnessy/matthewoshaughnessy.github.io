About me

I am a Ph.D. student in the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Georgia Tech. I'm very fortunate to be advised by Mark Davenport and Chris Rozell, and to be supported by the NDSEG Fellowship.

My broad research interests entail signal processing, machine learning and statistics. I am particularly interested in the role of structure and causality in statistical inference. My past and current work involves (1) Bayesian methods for tracking sparse signals; and (2) the use of low-dimensional models in theory and algorithms for causal inference.

Outside of research, I enjoy hiking and cycling, music (especially classical), reading, and cooking. My Erdös number is 4, but as an extra in a major movie I have a Bacon number of 2. This gives me an Erdös-Bacon number of 6 (without resorting to homophones like some shameless academics).
